## Basic Objects

A set is treated extensionally: two sets are equal when they have the same elements. A function
$
f : X \to Y
$
assigns to each point of the domain exactly one point of the codomain. For long-form geometry notes, the distinction between image, preimage, injection, surjection, and bijection is not cosmetic; it controls when structures can be pushed forward or pulled back.

For a subset $A \subseteq Y$, the preimage
$$
f^{-1}(A)=\{x\in X: f(x)\in A\}
$$
is defined without requiring $f$ to be invertible. This is why continuity is most naturally phrased using preimages of open sets.

## Relations

A relation on $X$ is a subset $R\subseteq X\times X$. An equivalence relation is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive. Equivalence classes form a quotient set $X/R$, and the quotient construction is the set-theoretic prototype for quotient spaces, covering spaces, and bundle constructions.

## Proof Style

Most notes in this site use a definition-first style:

1. State the object and the maps involved.
2. Check whether the property is local or global.
3. Track the direction of functions, especially for pullbacks.
4. Separate canonical statements from choices of coordinates, bases, gauges, or representatives.
