# The Big Picture

A fiber bundle is a mathematical device for describing the following situation:

> At each point of some base space, another space is attached. Locally the whole object looks like a product, but globally the attachment may be twisted.

The basic notation is 

$$
\pi:E\to B.
$$

 Here:

- $B$ is the **base space**. In physics this may be real space, spacetime, parameter space, or momentum space.

- $E$ is the **total space**. It contains all the attached fibers.

- $\pi$ is the **projection map**. It tells you which base point a point of $E$ lies above.

- The **fiber over $b\in B$** is 

$$
E_b:=\pi^{-1}(b).
$$


<div className="mainbox">
One-sentence version A fiber bundle is locally a product $U\times F$, but it may fail to be globally a product $B\times F$.
</div>
The main hierarchy is this: 

$$
\boxed{\text{topological fiber bundle}}
\quad \supset \quad
\boxed{\text{vector bundle}}
$$

 Vector bundles are fiber bundles whose fibers are vector spaces and whose transition functions are linear. Principal bundles are another important class of fiber bundles, where the fiber is a group $G$ and the bundle describes gauge frames.

<div className="center">
  Physical language                            Bundle language
  -------------------------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------------
  Vector field on a manifold                   Section of the tangent bundle $TM\to M$
  One-form such as $A=A_\mu dx^\mu$            Section of the cotangent bundle $T^*M\to M$
  Electromagnetic vector potential             Local expression of a connection on a $U(1)$ bundle
  Berry connection                             Connection on a Bloch vector bundle
  Berry curvature                              Curvature of that connection
  Chern number                                 Integral of a characteristic class
  Bloch occupied states $\{|u_a(k)\rangle\}$   Local frame for a vector bundle over the Brillouin zone
  Gauge transformation                         Change of local trivialization/frame
</div>
The note is written to prevent a common problem: if one jumps directly to Berry bundles or Chern numbers, the words "base space," "fiber," "section," and "connection" can feel like labels rather than definitions. We therefore build them in order.
